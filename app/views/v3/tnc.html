Terms and conditions for suppliers

These terms and conditions explain what you can expect from us, the Department for Education (DfE) and what we expect from you when using the Send data to DfE service. They should be read in conjunction with your existing user agreements and do not replace any agreements.

These terms refer only to the ‘Send data to DfE’ service and the data transferred using the service. We reserve the right to audit how you use this data and the service.

If you do not comply with any part of these terms, we reserve the right to remove your access (temporarily or permanently) with immediate effect.

By using this service you are agreeing to adhere to these terms and conditions and to comply with any associated DfE policies, guidance and standards.

What you can expect from us

We will:

  • provide the information and support you need to use this service
  • give you notice of any changes to the service or your access
  • provide a test environment
  • monitor transfers of data for auditing purposes
  • follow data protection law and security principles

What we expect from you

By registering to use ‘Send data to DfE’ you agree to comply with these terms and conditions.

We will:

  • act as data processors for customers, who remain the data controllers at all times
  • only process data through this service for the agreed scheduled transfer of data to and from the Department for Education

Data protection and security

You must follow data protection law and protect users in line with:

  • Data Protection Act 2018 - includes General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)
  • Privacy and Electronic Communications (EC Directive) Regulations 2003 – as amended
  • Equality Act 2010

You must also follow these acts and regulations if they’re changed or replaced.

You should follow the security principles of:

  • the National Cyber Security Centre
  • National Cyber Security Centre’s Guidance for secure development and deployment
  • National Cyber Security Centre’s Security Design Principles
  • the Open Web Application Security Project (OWASP)
  • Transport Layer Security principles for protecting data
  • Information Commissioner’s Office

You must:

  • keep your log-in and authentication details secure at all times
  • look out for and block suspicious attempts to access or manipulate user accounts
  • notify us immediately of any actual or suspected unauthorised use of your log-in or authentication details, account or other breaches of security, and details of any mitigation actions underway or planned
  • notify us immediately of any actual or suspected data breaches in relation to transferring data using 'Send data to DfE'
  • notify us if you are using the test environment for major testing in unusual ways, such as load testing, so that we don’t think this is abnormal behaviour
  • only use synthetic test data in the test environment
  • check software and releases for vulnerabilities through secure development and pre-release testing
  • validate and check any open source or proprietary code to check there are no vulnerabilities or malware

Processing and storing data

You must tell your users how you will use, transfer and store their data in relation to 'Send data to DfE'.

Any data transfers using 'Send data to DfE' should only be run from your registered product(s) and not any other system or platform.

When transferring data using 'Send data to DfE':

  • you are responsible for protecting your customers' data and for keeping this service secure
  • any transfer of data should be encrypted using Transport Layer Security standards (TLS 1.2 as a minimum, or equivalent), we will only accept data sent if it meets our encryption standards
  • you must not transfer or store data outside of the UK without DfE approval. We will need to get additional and detailed assurances from you about the data being processed and the security of your infrastructure and systems, as well as the personnel involved and data security law in the other country/counties
  • only store data for as long as is required to accurately provide the service
  • have processes in place to ensure that the data is not corrupted or lost and offer a way of restoring access to data in the event of a physical or technical incident
